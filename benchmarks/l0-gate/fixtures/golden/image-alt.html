Field notes: the red-tailed hawk

The red-tailed hawk is the most common large hawk across North America, and its rusty upper-tail is the field mark that gives the bird its name. The photograph below was taken at dawn from a ridge overlooking open grassland.

Hunting from a perch like this one, the hawk scans the field for the small mammals that make up the bulk of its diet before dropping in a fast, shallow stoop.
