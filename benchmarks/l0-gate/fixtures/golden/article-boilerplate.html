Understanding token budgets

Token budgets are the single most important lever when feeding web content into a language model. Every character you send costs money and, more importantly, context window space that could hold reasoning.

Why pruning wins

A typical article page ships thirty kilobytes of navigation, cookie banners, and analytics for every kilobyte of prose. Stripping that boilerplate before the model ever sees it routinely cuts the payload by ninety percent while preserving the argument the author actually made.

Measuring the result

The honest metric is compression against a full fetch of the same page. If your extractor claims a win it should be able to show the byte ratio on a real corpus, not a cherry-picked demo.
