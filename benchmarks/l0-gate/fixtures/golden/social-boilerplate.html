Why we rewrote the scheduler

The old scheduler was a single global queue guarded by one lock. It served us well for three years, but as the fleet grew past a thousand workers the lock became the bottleneck that capped our throughput no matter how much hardware we added.

The new design

We moved to per-shard queues with work stealing, so an idle worker pulls from a busy neighbour instead of contending on a global structure. Latency at the ninety-ninth percentile dropped by more than half in the first week of the rollout.
